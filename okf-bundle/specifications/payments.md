# Requirement (from the brief)

A payment gateway is to be **created using third-party software**, accessible **from the mobile app** by **members**. Members are charged for owning assets and for recurring maintenance of assets they own (see [Domain Model](/specifications/domain-model.md)); the gateway is how they settle those charges.

# Draft flow

1. Backend raises charges (one-time and recurring maintenance) per member per owned asset.
2. Member sees outstanding charges in the mobile app and initiates payment.
3. The gateway (Razorpay — see Provider below) processes the payment: UPI, cards, netbanking.
4. Gateway webhook confirms the payment to the backend; backend posts a receipt to the **Payment Ledger** and the **Bank Book** (see [Finance & Compliance](/specifications/finance-and-compliance.md)) and issues a receipt document to the member.
5. Offline payments (cash/cheque) are recorded by management and post to the Cash/Bank Book — the ledgers must not assume all receipts come via the gateway.

# Provider (decided 2026-07-20)

**Razorpay**, consumed through the provider-neutral payment interface at the backend boundary (which stays — switching providers must remain a backend-only change; **Cashfree** is the evaluated fallback).

| Criterion | Why Razorpay |
|---|---|
| Mobile | Mature native iOS/Android SDKs — fits the opaque `provider_params` handoff in the [Mobile Public API](/api/mobile/public-api.md) |
| Methods | UPI, cards, netbanking; UPI Autopay + e-NACH mandates available if recurring auto-debit is adopted |
| Per-society settlement | Partner/sub-merchant onboarding: **one merchant account per society**, KYC'd to the society, settling directly to the society's own bank account — no commingling through the vendor (fits single-society deployments, [Product Overview](/specifications/product-overview.md)) |
| Operations | Signed webhooks, RBI payment-aggregator license, broad documentation/ecosystem |

Merchant-account creation becomes a step of the society provisioning runbook, alongside [Society Directory](/architecture/society-directory.md) registration.

# Design implications (draft)

- Reconciliation is webhook-driven and idempotent; never mark a charge paid from the client alone.
- Receipts must be GST-compliant where applicable and stored as linked documents on the ledger entry.

# Further decisions (2026-07-20)

- **Recurring collection:** v1 is **member-initiated**, driven by push due-date reminders ([Mobile Public API](/api/mobile/public-api.md) device registration). UPI Autopay / e-NACH mandates are the designated fast-follow — Razorpay supports them, so no contract change will be needed beyond a mandate-setup endpoint.
- **Partial payments:** not allowed in v1 — a charge is settled in full (the contract already models `charge_ids` paid whole). **Late fees** are per-society configuration, generated by the scheduled charge run as **separate charges** — the original charge is never mutated.
- **Convenience fees:** UPI carries none (zero MDR on UPI P2M). Card/netbanking gateway fees are **passed to the member at checkout**, with a per-society toggle to absorb them instead.
